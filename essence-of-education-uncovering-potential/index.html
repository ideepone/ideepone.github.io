The Essence of Education: Uncovering Potential Within

Posted on 2022-04-01 Edited on 2024-08-26 In Times Word count in article: 611 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.
Explore the true essence of education through the metaphor of Michelangelo's David. Understand the roles of educators and learners in the journey of self-discovery and lifelong learning.

The Essence of Education: Uncovering Potential Within

A Profound Metaphor

A few days ago, I came across a short video that discussed a powerful question: “What is the essence of education?” The message was both simple and profound, and I’d like to share it with you.

When Michelangelo completed the statue of David, he was astounded, and so was everyone else.

A journalist asked Michelangelo, “How did you create such a magnificent work?”

He didn’t answer immediately. Instead, he pondered for a long time before carefully responding:

“It wasn’t I who created this great work; David was already inside the stone. I simply set him free.”

These words encapsulate the timeless essence of education:

“Every person has a better self, perhaps even a great self, within them. Every child undoubtedly possesses such a self.”

The Role of the Educator: A Guide to Discovery

When it comes to education, I believe it’s essential to consider two key roles: the educator and the learner.

For educators—whether they are teachers or mentors—encouragement and discovery should be at the forefront. Just like in Michelangelo’s story, the idea that “David” was already within the stone suggests that educators are not creators but guides. They play a vital role in helping to reveal the potential already present within each individual. The future will belong to the younger generation, as this is ingrained in the very fabric of our species.

Many educators, unfortunately, prefer to impose authority and control through their status, which can stifle creativity and self-expression. Young people have their own consciousness and view issues from different perspectives. It’s crucial to communicate with them as equals, focusing on the logic and substance of the matter at hand.

Educators hold their positions because they have taken steps ahead, possessing more experience or specialized skills. However, these “experiences” or “skills” are not infallible and should always be open to discussion and re-evaluation. Through this process, educators hope that learners will ultimately acquire and refine these experiences to create new value.

The Role of the Learner: Building Confidence

For learners, the most crucial element is “confidence”—the belief that they are unique and capable of greatness. However, confidence should be neither blind nor arrogant; it must be rooted in continuous self-awareness, learning, and a commitment to a scientific and dialectical approach to life.

I believe two insights are particularly important for learners:

  1. Success is Built on Resilience and Deliberate Practice
    The more valuable something is, the more effort and resistance it usually involves. I’ve always believed that easily obtained successes are rare; occasional success might come from sheer luck, but one cannot rely on such rare events. Instead, it’s essential to persist through failures, to enhance one’s abilities across multiple domains, and to strengthen the capacity for survival and growth. This ability requires constant learning and adaptation—lifelong learning for lifelong growth.

  2. Positive Psychological Feedback is Key
    Success is not always guaranteed, but it is often possible. Positive psychological feedback, such as praise and encouragement, can sometimes make the difference in persevering through difficult times. However, it’s equally important to recognize “sunk costs.” If too much time and effort have been invested in something without success, it may be wise to reconsider further investment—this is a common lesson in entrepreneurship.

The Path Forward: Self-Discovery and Beyond

Both educators and learners must first gain a clear understanding of themselves before they can effectively understand others and the organizations they are part of. The ultimate goal of education is to discover our true selves and to help uncover the “true self” in others, ensuring that human civilization continues to progress and evolve.
