习惯是个行为，行为更容易改变

Posted on 2022-04-29 Edited on 2024-08-15 In 时光集
Symbols count in article: 480 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

前两天抖音上看到《此念官方》的一个心理学方面的短视频《什么样的话最不应该对孩子说》，很不错。

嘉宾黄仕明这样回答的：在身份层面负面的语言（不会说）。

举个例子，如果我有一个个案跟我说，嘿，老师，我是一个酒鬼，他找我做心理咨询。

我不会认同的，我会说，嘿，你是说，你有喝酒的习惯吗？

习惯是个行为，行为更容易改变。

如果这个人说，我是一个抑郁的人。

我会这样说，你是说，你笑得比别人少一点吗？

如果我的孩子跟我说，爸爸，我肯定会失败的。身份认同，我不可能成功。

我会说，噢，你是说，这一次，你会失败吗？

让那个绷紧的地方（深吐气），打开一个放松的空间，在这个空间里才可能有新的东西进来。

当然，明天考试可能还是会失败的，但是我们之间有一个同盟，一起有一个意图，看到未来如何可以做得比现在好一点点，我成为了孩子眼里那个同盟，相信我的那个人。

———

很多时候，不管是给自己或者给他人很容易打上性质的标签，比如，我是一个loser，他是一个坏人等等。

可能因为这一个性质的标签，接下来若干年都在背负着这个无形的压力，甚至让人透不过气来，如果幸运，能走出来很好，但如果走不出来，可能就一直是个死循环。

人生是没有边界的，是没有那么多标签的，更不应该定性，这个世界也不是非黑即白，非好即坏的，很多时候时间是最好的陪伴，积极的心态会改变很多的。

Habit is a Behavior, and Behavior is Easier to Change

Posted on 2022-04-29 Edited on 2024-08-15 In Times
Symbols count in article: 300 Reading time ≈ 1 mins.

A few days ago, I came across a short psychology video on Douyin from “This Thought Official” titled “What Should You Never Say to Your Child?” It was quite insightful.

Guest Huang Shiming answered: Negative language at the identity level should not be used.

For example, if a client says to me, “Hey, teacher, I’m an alcoholic,” and seeks psychological counseling.

I won’t agree with that. I would say, “Hey, are you saying that you have a habit of drinking?”

Habit is a behavior, and behavior is easier to change.

If someone says, “I’m a depressed person.”

I would respond, “Are you saying you smile a little less than others?”

If my child says, “Dad, I’m sure I’ll fail. I identify as someone who can’t succeed.”

I would say, “Oh, are you saying that this time, you might fail?”

This relaxes the tension (deep breath) and opens up a space for relaxation, where new things can come in.

Of course, they might still fail the exam tomorrow, but now we share an alliance, a common intention, to see how things can get a little better than they are now. I become that ally in my child’s eyes, the person they believe in.

———

Many times, whether labeling ourselves or others, it’s easy to apply definitive labels, such as “I’m a loser,” or “He’s a bad person.”

Carrying such a definitive label can be an invisible burden for years, sometimes suffocating. If you’re lucky, you might overcome it, but if not, it can become a vicious cycle.

Life has no boundaries, there aren’t as many labels as we think, and things shouldn’t be so easily defined. The world isn’t just black and white, good or bad. Often, time is the best companion, and a positive mindset can change a lot.

我思，故我在

Posted on 2022-04-23 Edited on 2024-08-15 In 时光集
Symbols count in article: 875 Reading time ≈ 3 mins.

笛卡尔说过：“我思，故我在。”

很多年前我在知乎上问过一个问题，黑格尔说的「存在即合理」怎么理解，这么多年好像就是追寻一个问题，既然我的存在是合理的，那为什么我会存在，又是因为什么我才存在与不同。

人生有时候不过是很多个假设，这些假设可能是社会的，也可能是自我的，剩下的就是围绕这些假设无尽的探索与追寻。

这些天在读两本书《直击本质》和《思考的力量》，起源是我对「本质」这个概念的源头感兴趣了，我们大多时候都是希望看到事物的本质，但却很难看到本质，那为什么有的人能看到本质，有的人看不到呢？

大多人都知道思考很重要，什么独立思考、批判思考、深度思考、逆向思考，然后又会提炼很多种思维方式，可人类文明至今一直没能把思考这件事解释清楚，比如意识是什么？

《思考的力量》里面对概念有一句这样的解释，思考指的是对外界的一个或者多个对象进行分析和判断，并得出关于该对象的具体结论的思维过程。

我一直在思考的一个问题是「如何理解这个世界」？

一个从世界到个人的反映过程，一个从客观世界到主观世界的过程，一个从此岸到彼岸的过程，很难但值得一生去思考。

这个世界你看到的可能不是真的，需要不断的确定什么才是真的，有的人觉得无趣，但我觉得这个很有意义。

生物学上讲，思考本质上是神经细胞之间发生的神经冲动，这些神经冲动带来了反射和其他行为。

我们每天都在思考，大量的信息涌入让你被动地思考，遇到新的问题主动地思考，甚至好像有时候心不在焉无意识地思考。

那到底怎么思考，怎么让自己的思考更高效，怎么让自己的思考变成可执行可操作呢？

思考是没有尽头的，但是可以有维度的，有深度、广度的，思考也可以是有边界的，有阶段的，甚至思考可以自我反省，思考思考的思考，元思考。

有时候理解世界的规则很简单，从事物本身到大脑的具体反应，可就是这短短的一段路，要经历的太多太多，一个不留意可能就没了边际，无法达到你心中的彼岸。

人类文明得以延续在我看来是前人不断思考探索的结晶，有几时我也在想，当你懂得了前人伟人的思考方式，他们的意识形态在这些千千万万的概念关系中被完全拷贝了一份，那个具有跟他们一样思维的人，还是你吗？

叔本华说过，“思考的人，在精神王国中，等于一国的君主”，愿每一个懂得思考的人能主宰自己的世界，成为自己的王。

I Think, Therefore I Am

Posted on 2022-04-23 Edited on 2024-08-15 In Times
Symbols count in article: 499 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

Descartes once said, “I think, therefore I am.”

Many years ago, I asked a question on Zhihu about how to understand Hegel’s phrase “What is real is rational.” For years, it seems I’ve been chasing the question: If my existence is rational, then why do I exist, and what makes my existence different?

Life, at times, seems to be a series of hypotheses—some societal, some personal. What follows is an endless pursuit and exploration surrounding these assumptions.

Lately, I’ve been reading two books: The Essence and The Power of Thinking. My interest in the concept of “essence” sparked this journey. Most of us seek to understand the essence of things, but it’s often elusive. So why can some people perceive the essence, while others cannot?

Most people recognize the importance of thinking—independent thinking, critical thinking, deep thinking, and reverse thinking. Yet, despite various thought processes being distilled over time, humanity has never fully explained the act of thinking itself, such as what consciousness is.

The Power of Thinking offers this definition of thinking: Thinking refers to the cognitive process of analyzing and judging one or more external objects to arrive at specific conclusions about them.

One question I’ve pondered for a long time is, “How can we understand the world?”

It’s a process that reflects the world onto the individual, moving from the objective world to the subjective, and from this shore to the other shore. It’s challenging but worth a lifetime of thought.

What you see in the world may not be true. It requires continuous verification to determine what is real. Some find this pursuit dull, but I find it deeply meaningful.

Biologically, thinking is essentially the result of nerve impulses between neurons, leading to reflexes and other behaviors.

We think every day, passively as information floods in, actively when faced with new problems, and sometimes unconsciously, even when our minds seem to wander.

But how should we think? How can we make our thinking more efficient? How can we turn our thoughts into actions?

While thinking may have no end, it can have dimensions—depth, breadth, boundaries, and stages. Thinking can even become reflexive, where we think about thinking itself—meta-thinking.

Sometimes understanding the rules of the world is simple, just a short journey from the object itself to the brain’s specific response. Yet this brief path is fraught with challenges, and one misstep could lead you astray, away from your intended destination.

In my view, human civilization persists because it is the crystallization of continuous thought and exploration by those who came before us. Sometimes I wonder, when you fully understand the thought processes of past greats, is that person who thinks just like them still you?

Schopenhauer said, “The man who thinks stands as the sovereign of his own spiritual kingdom.” May every thoughtful person reign over their own world and become the ruler of their own life.

配角还是主角？

Posted on 2022-04-20 Edited on 2024-08-13 In 时光集

「角色」

人生其实是一场又一场的角色扮演之旅。

很多人第一次蜕变的经历是突然知道了，原本以为自己是故事的主角，不过是个配角，甚至只是个路人而已。

小时候看电影喜欢看主演，慢慢得看电影除了看主角，更喜欢看配角，了解小角色的成长经历。

如果人生是一场戏，导演就只给你10秒钟，你会怎么把握呢？

我们一生其实会扮演很多个角色，可很多时候我们并不了解角色，甚至冥冥中很多角色是被突然安排过来的。

出生下来成为儿子女儿，上学成为学生，工作成为职员，然后成为高管老板，然后恋爱了结婚了，成为情人爱人，然后成为了爷爷奶奶外公外婆，抑或成为很多很多不同的角色。

可真的能明白每一个角色本身背后的意义和责任呢？

我工作中对角色感悟最深的经历是第一次当总监的时候，升职加薪内心是高兴的，可当真正开展工作中，对上对下的管理，前面几个月真的是糟糕透顶，很多事还是喜欢亲力亲为，也很难把握轻重缓急，老板说你，手下还顶撞你，团队被带得晕头转向的。

后来有一天突然明白了，到底「总监」是做什么的，这个角色是一个什么样的角色，然后清晰的把边界分清楚，专注做本质工作慢慢就好起来了。如果很难一下把角色换过来，自己和同事都会很累的。

什么是员工，什么是管理者？

真正的管理者，是你愿意主动的去思考、去担当、去引导、去行动，而不是命令，你真正做得好的能力有多少可能比得过一线的专业者呢。

那生活中呢，什么是子女和父母，什么是学生和老师，什么是朋友，什么是爱人，这些角色的边界到底在哪儿呢，又期望一个与对方交往的时候是一个什么角色呢？

有太多的时候，我们是角色混淆了，或者是角色不清晰，是被动的入选了某个角色，其实可以主动的担当起来。

前阵子网上有个短视频，在国外的一个战争游戏中，红蓝双方抢夺阵地，其中一方节节败退，最后指挥官直接下线跑了，这个时候一个中国玩家主动出来中英双语指挥，带领着大家最后走向胜利。

也许这个中国玩家开始玩只是简单的想扮演一个士兵，但关键时刻却能主动承担指挥官角色并且完美地胜任下来。

这里面最主要的一点是日常「用心」，用心去观察，用心去思考，敢于尝试和担当，关键时刻才能胜任。

人生很多角色就是被安排的，那就好好的把这个角色扮演好，如果有一天导演给了一个10秒的角色，你会把握住吗？

王晶评价周星驰在大话西游里面说10秒换了7个表情，这7个表情背后又付出了多少呢？

Supporting Role or Leading Role?

Posted on 2022-04-20 Edited on 2024-08-15 In Times
Symbols count in article: 607 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

Life is essentially a journey of playing different roles.

Many people’s first experience of transformation is the sudden realization that, though they thought they were the main character in their story, they were actually just a supporting role, or even merely a passerby.

As a child, I used to enjoy watching the main characters in movies. But as I grew older, I found myself more interested in the supporting roles, learning about the growth experiences of the smaller characters.

If life were a play, and the director only gave you 10 seconds, how would you seize that moment?

We play many roles throughout our lives, but often, we don’t fully understand these roles. Some roles seem to be handed to us unexpectedly.

We are born as sons or daughters, we go to school and become students, then we work and become employees, eventually rising to executive roles. We fall in love, get married, and become lovers, then eventually grandparents or take on many other roles.

But do we truly understand the meaning and responsibility behind each role?

One of my deepest reflections on roles came when I was first promoted to director. I was excited about the promotion and the raise, but when I started working in that role, managing both up and down the chain was incredibly challenging. For the first few months, I struggled, trying to handle everything personally, unable to prioritize effectively. My boss criticized me, my subordinates pushed back, and my team was in disarray.

Then one day, I suddenly understood what being a “director” really meant. I grasped the nature of the role, set clear boundaries, and focused on the essential tasks. Slowly, things started to improve. If you can’t quickly adapt to a new role, both you and your colleagues will feel exhausted.

What does it mean to be an employee? What does it mean to be a manager?

A true manager is someone who is willing to think proactively, take responsibility, guide others, and take action, rather than just giving orders. How many of your skills as a manager could actually surpass those of the specialists on the front line?

In life, what does it mean to be a child or a parent? A student or a teacher? A friend or a lover? Where are the boundaries of these roles, and what do we expect from the other person in each relationship?

Too often, we confuse our roles or lack clarity in them, or we passively fall into a role without taking it on actively.

Recently, there was a viral short video about a war game played abroad. The red and blue teams were battling for territory, and one side was retreating steadily. In the end, their commander logged off and fled. At that moment, a Chinese player stepped up and took over the command, leading the team to victory with bilingual English-Chinese instructions.

This Chinese player may have started the game simply wanting to play as a soldier, but when the moment came, they took on the role of commander and performed it perfectly.

The key here is “attentiveness” in daily life—paying attention, thinking carefully, daring to try and take responsibility. Only then can you rise to the occasion when it counts.

Many roles in life are assigned to us. If that’s the case, then let’s play those roles well. If one day the director gives you a 10-second role, will you seize it?

Wong Jing once remarked that Stephen Chow showed seven different expressions in a 10-second scene in A Chinese Odyssey. How much effort do you think went into perfecting those seven expressions?

我就是我，是颜色不一样的烟火

Posted on 2022-04-16 Edited on 2024-08-15
Symbols count in article: 933 Reading time ≈ 3 mins.

「逻辑·概念」

《形式逻辑》说：概念是反映事物的「特有」属性（固有属性或本质属性）的思维形态。

最近一直在探寻一个问题，到底个人有没有跃迁的方式方法？

学习精进、笔记整理、知识体系构建、认知觉醒等等，可是始终感觉都无法让自己感到内心的平静，每天的问题仍然好像解决不完，始终疑惑，始终在探索。

工作中上下级的沟通，好像这事你说得很明白，但最终的结果却相差不少，最终一复盘讨论，大家对一些概念事物本身的理解就有偏差。

有一天看书读到爱因斯坦的那句：这个世界的最不可理解之处就在于它是可以理解的。有可能是长时间被一些问题困惑着，突然听到「理解」这个词，瞬间被感动的一塌糊涂。

后来发现，理解这世界的一种方式是「逻辑」，是归纳和演绎，并且构成逻辑的基础是「概念」。

于是，这个世界的理解范式在我看来，其实变得很简单了，你有没有真正理解一个「概念」。

概念最重要的一个点是「特性」，是区分于其他事物的关键因素，这样会发现很多问题很好理解了。

互联网经常提到，如果你的产品不能用一句话说明白，就不是一个合格的产品，商业里其实是一个「定位」问题，逻辑里不过是一个「概念」描述，一个「下定义」的过程。

很多人说自己不会表达，不会表达的前提是没有想清楚，一团乱麻，没有想清楚之所以乱麻的是因为逻辑没梳理清楚，而逻辑不清晰，很可能是因为很多「概念」不清晰。

我现在喜欢一种新的记录Flomo方式，先只记「关键词」，这种方式快捷高效，一方面是一条线索也便于索引，另外一方面其实让自己去刻意练习逻辑的两个能力：「演绎」和「抽象」，抽空的时候再把这个概念演绎出来，甚至产生一篇小报童。

演绎是怎么把一个概念发散，讲得更好理解，可以下定义，可以举例子论证；抽象是怎么把一个复杂的事简单化，把共性提炼出来，尽可能的言简意赅，这其实是结构化表达的基础。

去年笔记侠年会柯洲老师专门提到「概念思维」，这是一种思维方式，查了一下，华为的「苏关哲」老师也经常提到。

概念思维是一种识别表面上没有明显联系的事情之间内部联系本质特征的能力。

从这个概念来讲，我的理解是一种透过现象看本质的思维能力，但好像还是没有说的很清楚，因为透过现象看本质的思维方式可能很多，这不是「概念思维」的特性，为此我还跟柯洲老师提了几个问题，等什么时候回复有了新答案再更新下。

我个人认为，试着理解世界的一种方式就是理解万事万物的概念，怎么去理解概念，就是怎么去了解这个世界。

张国荣唱：「我就是我，是颜色不一样的烟火」。

「我」如果想活得精彩独立，其实是要有自己的特性，个性也好，价值输出也好，要有与众不同的那面，就会明白「自我」了。

I Am What I Am, a Firework of a Different Color

Posted on 2022-04-16 Edited on 2024-08-15 In Times
Symbols count in article: 562 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

Formal Logic states that a concept is a form of thought that reflects the “unique” attributes (inherent or essential properties) of an object.

Recently, I’ve been exploring a question: Is there a way for personal breakthrough?

Continuous learning, organizing notes, building knowledge systems, cognitive awakening, and so on. Yet, I still feel unsettled, as if daily problems remain unsolved, always in doubt, always exploring.

In work communication between superiors and subordinates, it often seems like you’ve explained something clearly, but the final result differs significantly. When we reflect on it, we often find that there were discrepancies in how we understood certain concepts or things.

One day, while reading, I came across Einstein’s statement: “The most incomprehensible thing about the world is that it is comprehensible.” Having been puzzled by some issues for a long time, I was deeply moved by the word “comprehensible.”

Later, I realized that one way to understand the world is through “logic,” through induction and deduction, and the foundation of logic is “concepts.”

Thus, the paradigm for understanding the world, in my view, becomes quite simple: Have you truly understood a “concept”?

The most crucial aspect of a concept is its “characteristic,” the key factor that distinguishes it from other things. This understanding makes many problems much clearer.

The internet often mentions that if you can’t explain your product in one sentence, it’s not a qualified product. In business, this is about “positioning,” while in logic, it’s about “concept” description, a process of “definition.”

Many people say they can’t express themselves well. The reason for this is often that they haven’t thought things through; it’s a mess. The reason for this mess is unclear logic, and unclear logic is likely due to unclear “concepts.”

I now enjoy a new way of note-taking with Flomo: just recording “keywords.” This method is quick and efficient. On one hand, it provides a clue and facilitates indexing; on the other hand, it helps practice two logical abilities: “deduction” and “abstraction.” Later, when I have time, I can elaborate on these concepts, possibly leading to a small blog post.

Deduction involves expanding on a concept, making it more understandable, defining it, or providing examples. Abstraction involves simplifying complex matters, extracting commonalities, and being as concise as possible, which is the foundation of structured expression.

At last year’s NoteMan Annual Conference, Teacher Ke Zhou specifically mentioned “conceptual thinking,” a way of thinking. I looked it up and found that Huawei’s Teacher Su Guanzhe also frequently discusses it.

Conceptual Thinking is the ability to identify the essential characteristics of seemingly unrelated things.

In this sense, my understanding is that it is a thinking ability that sees the essence through phenomena. But it seems I haven’t explained it very clearly because there are many ways to see the essence through phenomena. This isn’t the unique trait of “conceptual thinking.” I’ve asked Teacher Ke Zhou some questions about this, and I’ll update when I receive new answers.

Personally, I believe that trying to understand the world is about understanding the concepts of all things. Understanding concepts is understanding the world.

Leslie Cheung sings, “I am what I am, a firework of a different color.”

If “I” want to live a brilliant and independent life, I need to have my own characteristics, whether it’s individuality or value output. Having that unique aspect helps you understand “self.”

生命以负熵为生，保持痛感

Posted on 2022-04-15 Edited on 2024-08-13 In 时光集
Symbols count in article: 220 Reading time ≈ 1 mins.

「痛感」

痛感指痛苦的感觉或深切地感动。

成长是一个不断走出舒适区的过程，长久地待在舒适区，会让人失去思考的习惯，会让人逃避现实，是一种缺少危机意识的行为。

薛定谔说过：“人活着就是在对抗熵增定律，生命以负熵为生”。

这是我最喜欢的一句话，悲观而浪漫。

保持痛感是对生命生活的一种敬畏，生命本身就是「向死而生」，生活本就是「无常」，只有真正明白了这些才会从内心迸发出一种向生的力量感。

看电影、听音乐、阅读、锻炼等等很多种方式，让内心和身体都可以处在一个「心跳」的状态。

保持痛感，保持感悟，向阳而生，未来才会越来越精彩。

上海疫情封闭的这些天

Posted on 2022-04-08 Edited on 2024-08-13 In 时光集
Symbols count in article: 668 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

「无常」

世人都在求安定，无奈世事总是无常。

上海疫情看似来得很突然，连封城好像都猝不及防，每天都有新增感染者，原定5号解封的，今天8号还在待定，很多人囤粮都没了，甚至压根儿就没囤，社会的快递外卖等派送力又不够，小区居委会的组织管理一下又没跟上来，若大的上海好像乱糟糟的。

不确定的大环境之下，其实最重要的是抗风险能力，反脆弱的能力。

「每一个事物都会从波动中得到利益或蒙受损失，脆弱性是指因为波动和不确定而承受损失，反脆弱性则是指让自己避免这些损失，甚至从混乱中不确定中获利。」

首先，我觉得反脆弱的能力重要的一项是「心态」，积极乐观的心态是非常重要的，就像《反脆弱》书中也提到尼采的那句话：“杀不死我的，只会让我更强大。”

这场抗疫中，有不少人在抱怨在吐槽，说这也不行那也乱，而有担当的人早已置身一线分析问题解决问题。

大多困难面前失败的根本原因是自己的意志消沉和心态的崩塌。

其次，我觉得要有「危机意识」。

比如囤粮，有的家庭就会囤不少，这个囤不是疯狂抢购，是有节制的囤。如果5号说解封，如果不解封，你会怎么做，我至少多囤点，以防万一。囤一些必要持久的，如米油盐蛋等。

我一直相信一个原则，任何事情要做最坏的打算。

工作中，你的团队告诉你项目进展10号能完成，你自己心理要有预期，如果10号完不成怎么办，你是否要预留时间，或者提前赶进度。

新冠疫情的这几年，多少行业公司都无法坚持下来直至倒闭关门，而新东方却反而能活下来，俞敏洪从第一天开始就做好了这个准备。

德州扑克其实很锻炼这个，都是概率，偶尔赌一赌可以，但如果总是入池，或者动辄All in，结果只有输。

无常其实是常态，影响事情的发展因素是多维的，是概率的，百分百太难了，要学会的是在无常和不确定性下很好的生存发展，这是一项基本能力。

可以读读《反脆弱》，也深入了解一下这项能力。

Uncertainty

Posted on 2022-04-08 Edited on 2024-08-15 In Times
Symbols count in article: 485 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

People everywhere seek stability, yet the world is often unpredictable.

The COVID-19 outbreak in Shanghai seemed sudden, and the lockdown appeared to come out of nowhere. Every day, new cases emerge, and the lockdown, originally scheduled to end on the 5th, is still in question on the 8th. Many people have run out of stockpiled food, or never had any to begin with. The delivery services, such as couriers and takeout, are overwhelmed, and neighborhood committees are struggling to keep up with organizing and managing the community. It feels like chaos in a city as large as Shanghai.

In an uncertain environment, the most crucial thing is the ability to manage risk—the ability to be antifragile.

“Every entity benefits from or is harmed by volatility. Fragility is the condition of being harmed by uncertainty and volatility, while antifragility is the ability to avoid these harms and even benefit from chaos and uncertainty.”

First and foremost, I believe that an important aspect of antifragility is “mindset.” A positive and optimistic attitude is vital. As mentioned in the book Antifragile, there’s a famous quote by Nietzsche: “That which does not kill me makes me stronger.”

In this battle against the pandemic, while many people are complaining and criticizing, those with responsibility have already stepped up to analyze and solve problems on the front lines.

In most cases, the fundamental reason for failure in the face of difficulties is the collapse of one’s will and mindset.

Secondly, I believe it’s essential to have a “crisis awareness.”

Take stockpiling food, for instance. Some families stock up a considerable amount—not through panic buying, but in a controlled manner. If the lockdown was expected to end on the 5th but didn’t, what would you do? At the very least, you would stockpile more essentials like rice, oil, salt, and eggs just in case.

I’ve always believed in one principle: prepare for the worst in any situation.

At work, if your team tells you a project will be completed by the 10th, you should mentally prepare for the possibility that it might not be. Do you have backup time? Should you push the progress ahead?

Over the past few years of the COVID-19 pandemic, many companies across various industries have struggled to survive, with many eventually closing down. But New Oriental managed to stay afloat because Yu Minhong prepared for this from day one.

Playing Texas Hold’em is a good exercise for this mindset; it’s all about probabilities. Occasionally taking risks is fine, but if you constantly go all-in, the result is inevitably a loss.

Uncertainty is, in fact, the norm. The factors influencing outcomes are multidimensional and probabilistic; achieving 100% certainty is nearly impossible. What we need to learn is how to thrive and grow in the face of uncertainty and unpredictability. This is a fundamental skill.

Consider reading Antifragile and delving deeper into this ability.

蓦然回首，那人却在灯火阑珊处

Posted on 2022-04-04 Edited on 2024-08-23 In 时光集
Symbols count in article: 599 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

「境界·山水」

最近谈到「升维」，其实是一种境界的提升。

我非常喜欢的一段禅语：

「看山是山，看水是水；

看山不是山，看水不是水；

看山还是山，看水还是水。」

人生的很多转角阶段，我都喜欢把这句话拿来细细地咀嚼。学生时代读到这句话时，我能懂，但逐渐发现也只是停留在能懂的层面。活了小半生，经历种种，原来很多道理背后的五味杂陈其实是「经历」。

新生事物的出现，第一反应通常是看到表象，这就是山，这就是水。也许好奇心会主动探寻，或许这些事物被动地闯入你的生活中。

当你仔细了解事物的特性时，可能会发现它与原来的认知有所不同。

生活中有一个很好的例子：我们从小学了这么多汉字，在段落句子中汉字可能辨识无误，但有一天突然把某个字单独拿出来，竟然觉得有些陌生，甚至怀疑自己是否写错了。

生活和工作中的很多事情也一样。有时你会觉得一份工作只是工作，但当你真的去做时，能否做到优秀？能否坚持年复一年，日复一日？加入时间的维度后，这些事情可能变得不那么简单，你或许会重新认识它们。

当你开始认真对待一件事；当你深度剖析，从头到尾，从内到外；甚至把一件事当作终生坚持的伴侣时，你会变得越来越从容。

王国维先生也概括过人生的三重境界：

  1. 昨夜西风凋碧树，独上高楼，望尽天涯路。
  2. 衣带渐宽终不悔，为伊消得人憔悴。
  3. 众里寻他千百度，蓦然回首，那人却在灯火阑珊处。

中国人谈境界，往往喜欢说那是只可意会，不可言传的，可能是感受属于自身，只有自己体会才能知晓其中滋味。

禅宗六祖慧能的偈语中说：

「菩提本无树，明镜亦非台；

本来无一物，何处惹尘埃。」

也许当你游历山水之后，回首时会发现，一切不过是云淡风轻，便是圆满了吧。

Suddenly Looking Back, That Person Is at the Lantern Festival

Posted on 2022-04-04 Edited on 2024-08-23 In Times
Symbols count in article: 472 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.

“State·Landscape”

Recently, discussions about “higher dimensions” often refer to an elevation in one’s state of mind.

One of my favorite Zen sayings is:

“Seeing a mountain is just seeing a mountain, seeing water is just seeing water;

Seeing a mountain is no longer just a mountain, seeing water is no longer just water;

Seeing a mountain is once again seeing a mountain, seeing water is once again seeing water.”

At various turning points in life, I like to reflect on this saying. When I first read it during my school years, I understood it, but gradually realized that I only grasped it on a superficial level. After living for half my life and experiencing many things, I found that much of the wisdom behind it comes from “experience.”

When new things appear, our first reaction is often to see only the surface—this is a mountain, this is water. Curiosity might drive us to explore, or these things might simply intrude into our lives.

When you delve deeper into the characteristics of these things, you might discover they differ from your initial perception.

A good example from life: We have learned so many Chinese characters since elementary school. While recognizing characters in paragraphs and sentences is straightforward, seeing a single character in isolation might suddenly feel unfamiliar, even leading to doubts about whether it’s written correctly.

Many aspects of life and work are similar. What seems like a simple job might become more complex when actually undertaken. Can you do it well? Can you maintain it year after year, day after day? When time is added into the equation, things might become less straightforward, and you may find yourself re-evaluating them.

When you start to seriously address a matter; when you analyze it thoroughly from beginning to end, from inside to out; and even when you take it on as a lifelong pursuit, you will become increasingly at ease.

Mr. Wang Guowei also summarized the three realms of life:

  1. Last night’s west wind withered the green tree; climbing the high tower alone, gazing at the road to the horizon.
  2. The belt grows wider, yet no regret; I have withered away for her.
  3. Searching for him a thousand times in the crowd, suddenly looking back, that person is at the lantern festival.

In Chinese discussions of states, people often say that they are only to be felt and not explained. This may be because the experience is personal, and only one’s own experience can reveal its true essence.

The Zen Master Huineng said:

“Without a Bodhi tree, and the bright mirror is not a platform;

Originally, there is nothing, where can dust cling?”

Perhaps after you have traveled through landscapes, looking back, you will find that it is all but a gentle cloud, which is the essence of completeness.

教育的本质

Posted on 2022-04-01 Edited on 2024-08-13 In 时光集
Symbols count in article: 961 Reading time ≈ 3 mins.

「教育」

前两天看到一个短视频讲「教育的本质是什么」，我觉得挺好，分享出来：

———

当年米开朗基罗做成大卫之后，自己也觉得震撼，所有人都震撼。

记者就问米开朗基罗，您是怎么创作出如此伟大的作品？

他没有轻易地回答，他说让我想一想，他想了半天之后很慎重的回答：

「不是我创造出这么伟大的作品，是那个石头里头本来就有个大卫，我只是把它释放出来。」

这话太精彩了，这话才是千古教育的本质。

「每个人身体里都有一个更好的自我，甚至是伟大的自我，每个孩子里都一定有这样一个自我。」

———

对于教育来讲，我觉得需要从两个角色看：教者和学者。

教者，老师也好，长辈也好，我喜欢的是鼓励和发掘的方式，就像视频里面说的，那个石头里面本来就有一个「大卫」，对于教者我们应该首先相信自己只不过是一个引路人，扮演的是一个传承的角色，未来一定是年轻人的天下，这是人类物种写在基因里面的。

很多教者喜欢用命令的方式，以身份来压制，这种很不好，年轻人是有自我意识的，是会从不同的角度思考问题的，我们应当以平等的方式去沟通，去针对事情本身的逻辑来沟通。

教者之所以是教者，很大可能是我们先人一步而已，有更多的过往经验；抑或是我们在某些方面的能力更强更专业，但这些「经验」或「能力」并不一定是对的，我们需要拿出来探讨。也正是因为这些，教者希望学者最终学到这些经验，以创造更多的价值。

而对于学者，其实最重要的一点是「自信」，要相信自己是独一无二的，是优秀的，当然自信不是盲目的，不能自负。这种自信也是建立在自我认知不断学习的基础上，建立在科学辩证的认知态度之上的。

我觉得两点认知很重要：

一是成功是建立在多次失败的改进基础之上，是需要刻意练习的。

越是有价值意义的事情，其实背后付出就可能越多，阻力就越大，这个世界我一直认为轻而易举获得的事情太少，偶尔有只是幸运值爆棚，但人生不能完全寄托于小概率事件，是需要不断的经历尝试，让自己在多个领域的全方位能力越来越强，越来越有生存发展的能力，这种能力是需要不断的与时俱进，不断地学习，活到老学到老，学到老活到老。

二是自己和环境给予的心理正向反馈，比如赞美、鼓励。

很多时候事情并不是百分百能成功，只是可能会成功，但积极心理学的潜意识某种程度是可以让自己坚持下来的，坚持坚持就成功了。不过有时候也要考虑「沉没成本」，就是如果事情付出太久太多仍然没有成功，是不是考虑要再过多的去投入了，创业时很容易犯这种错。

教者也好、学者也好，首先应该对自我的认知越来越清晰，其次对他人、对组织的认知也越来越清晰，要发现那个真正的自我，也要学会挖掘他人的「真我」，才可以让人类的文明得以传承下去。
