Habit is a Behavior, and Behavior is Easier to Change

Posted on 2022-04-29 Edited on 2024-08-15 In Times
Symbols count in article: 300 Reading time ≈ 1 mins.

A few days ago, I came across a short psychology video on Douyin from “This Thought Official” titled “What Should You Never Say to Your Child?” It was quite insightful.

Guest Huang Shiming answered: Negative language at the identity level should not be used.

For example, if a client says to me, “Hey, teacher, I’m an alcoholic,” and seeks psychological counseling.

I won’t agree with that. I would say, “Hey, are you saying that you have a habit of drinking?”

Habit is a behavior, and behavior is easier to change.

If someone says, “I’m a depressed person.”

I would respond, “Are you saying you smile a little less than others?”

If my child says, “Dad, I’m sure I’ll fail. I identify as someone who can’t succeed.”

I would say, “Oh, are you saying that this time, you might fail?”

This relaxes the tension (deep breath) and opens up a space for relaxation, where new things can come in.

Of course, they might still fail the exam tomorrow, but now we share an alliance, a common intention, to see how things can get a little better than they are now. I become that ally in my child’s eyes, the person they believe in.

———

Many times, whether labeling ourselves or others, it’s easy to apply definitive labels, such as “I’m a loser,” or “He’s a bad person.”

Carrying such a definitive label can be an invisible burden for years, sometimes suffocating. If you’re lucky, you might overcome it, but if not, it can become a vicious cycle.

Life has no boundaries, there aren’t as many labels as we think, and things shouldn’t be so easily defined. The world isn’t just black and white, good or bad. Often, time is the best companion, and a positive mindset can change a lot.
