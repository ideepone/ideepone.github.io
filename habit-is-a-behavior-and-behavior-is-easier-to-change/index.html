Habit is a Behavior, and Behavior is Easier to Change

Posted on 2022-04-29 Edited on 2024-08-26 In Times
Symbols count in article: 290 Reading time ≈ 1 mins.
Explore how labeling and defining oneself or others can limit potential and how shifting to view habits as behaviors can facilitate change.

Habit is a Behavior, and Behavior is Easier to Change

A few days ago, I came across a thought-provoking psychology video on Douyin from “This Thought Official” titled “What Should You Never Say to Your Child?” The insights shared were quite impactful.

Guest Huang Shiming highlighted the importance of avoiding negative language at the identity level. For instance, if a client says, “Hey, teacher, I’m an alcoholic,” seeking psychological counseling, my response would be different. Instead of agreeing with the label, I would say, “Are you saying that you have a habit of drinking?”

Habit is a behavior, and behavior is easier to change.

Similarly, if someone says, “I’m a depressed person,” my response would be, “Are you saying you smile a little less than others?”

If my child expresses doubt with statements like, “Dad, I’m sure I’ll fail. I identify as someone who can’t succeed,” I would counter with, “Oh, are you saying that this time, you might fail?”

This approach helps to relax the tension and opens up a space for improvement and new possibilities. Even if the exam doesn’t go well, the focus shifts to a supportive alliance, aiming for gradual improvement. This perspective helps build trust and support.

The Impact of Labeling

Often, definitive labels such as “I’m a loser” or “He’s a bad person” can impose a heavy, invisible burden. These labels can be suffocating and, if not addressed, may create a vicious cycle of self-fulfilling prophecies.

Life is not confined by rigid boundaries, and we shouldn’t be limited by simplistic definitions. The world is complex and nuanced, with more shades than just black and white. Embracing this complexity and maintaining a positive mindset can lead to significant changes over time.
