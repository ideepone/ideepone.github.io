The Dao in the Balance of Yin and Yang

The ancient saying goes: “The Dao is the balance of Yin and Yang.”

The quest for perfection and persistent self-reflection can validate our existence and lead to profound self-awareness.

In developing my personal knowledge system using Flomo, I have experienced several stages:

  1. Classic Notes: Minimal reflection, random keyword tags, no classification.
  2. Increased Reflection: Less note-taking, categorized tags [P.A.R.A], structured expression.
  3. Contrasting Thinking: Examining facts vs. opinions, rational vs. emotional, objective vs. subjective, explicit vs. implicit, self vs. others, individual vs. society, ordered vs. disordered, positive vs. negative, etc.
  4. Form and Content: Reflecting on form vs. content, logical thinking, tracing philosophy, and conceptual thinking.
  5. Exploration and Simplification: Contemplating the present vs. the beyond, exploring simplicity, pursuing an ideal realm.
  6. Abstract and Deductive: Thinking in abstract vs. deductive terms.
  7. …

Currently, my reflections have yet to reach the ideal pinnacle of Dao or the metaphorical boundary of understanding. Reading the profound insights of ancient sages fills me with immense joy and a deep sense of gratitude.

In Season 2 of KQ Walk the World, there is a hypothesis: What if deserts disappeared from Earth?

Transforming deserts into forests might seem like a grand achievement, but deserts play a crucial role in the global climate cycle. Removing them could potentially create new ecological problems for the Earth.

Are justice and evil absolute? Is right and wrong truly important? Is there a gray area between black and white? Are rational numbers finite? Do irrational numbers have an end? Why is energy conserved? Why does entropy continue to increase?

The world of computers consists of only 0 and 1, while the real world is a balance of Yin and Yang, constantly rotating and alternating.

Perhaps the boundary between reality and virtuality is merely a matter of perception. What defines the point where quantity transforms into quality?
